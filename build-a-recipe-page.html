Kacchi

Kacchi Biryani is a rich and aromatic dish popular in Bangladesh, made with marinated mutton, fragrant basmati rice, and a blend of traditional spices. Unlike regular biryani, the raw marinated meat is layered with rice and slow-cooked on dum (steam) for hours, allowing the flavors to meld perfectly. It is often garnished with fried onions, saffron-infused milk, and boiled potatoes, making it a festive favorite at weddings and special occasions. Served with salad and borhani, Kacchi Biryani is a true indulgence for biryani lovers.

Ingredients

  • Mutton (Goat Meat)
  • Basmati or Chinigura Rice
  • Yogurt & Spices
  • Ghee & Saffron

Instructions

  1. Marinate the Meat – Marinate mutton with yogurt, spices, fried onions, and mustard oil for at least 4–6 hours or overnight.
  2. Layer with Rice – In a heavy-bottomed pot, layer the marinated meat with soaked basmati rice, saffron milk, and ghee.
  3. Seal and Cook – Seal the pot with dough and cook on low heat (dum) for 1.5–2 hours until the meat is tender.
  4. Fluff and Serve – Gently mix the biryani, ensuring even flavors, and serve hot with salad and raita.
